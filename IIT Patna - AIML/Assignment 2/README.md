# 🎙️ BERT Research Paper – Audio Overview using NotebookLM

## ✨ Overview

This project demonstrates how AI can be used to simplify and understand complex research papers through audio-based learning.

I selected the research paper on **BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers)** from Google Scholar and uploaded it into NotebookLM.

Using NotebookLM’s **Audio Overview** feature, the research paper was converted into an easy-to-understand conversational audio summary, making technical concepts more accessible and engaging.

This project highlights the use of AI tools for learning, research understanding, and voice-based knowledge delivery.

---

## 📘 Research Paper Chosen

### Paper Title:

**BERT: A Review of Applications in Natural Language
Processing and Understanding**

### Authors:

Koroteev M.V

### Source:

Google Scholar

## 🧠 Objective

The goal of this assignment was to:

* Explore how AI can simplify technical research papers
* Understand the core concepts behind BERT
* Use NotebookLM to generate an audio explanation
* Experience voice-based learning for academic content

Instead of reading the full paper manually, the Audio Overview feature provided a faster and more engaging way to understand the research.

---

## 🚀 Steps Followed

### Step 1: Research Paper Selection

I searched for the BERT research paper using Google Scholar and selected the original paper on language understanding using transformers.

---

### Step 2: Uploading to NotebookLM

I logged into NotebookLM and uploaded the research paper PDF.

NotebookLM processed the document and created an AI-powered knowledge space for the paper.

---

### Step 3: Audio Overview Generation

Using the **Audio Overview** feature, I asked NotebookLM to generate a spoken summary of the paper.

The tool created a natural conversational-style audio explanation of:

* What BERT is
* Why it was introduced
* How transformers work
* How BERT improved NLP tasks
* Its importance in modern AI systems

---

### Step 4: Saving the Audio Output

The generated audio file was saved and added to GitHub as part of the project documentation.

This allows others to listen to the summary instead of only reading the research paper.
